Welcome

To Big Bamboo, located on Vessup Bay in Red Hook, a short walking distance from the ferry dock. It's where The Caribbean Sea & the Atlantic meet! You'll find palm trees and mangroves surrounding the bay. This natural safe harbor for vessels, was used by many sailors for generations to their boats every night or for extended stays.

There's a few small pocket beaches tucked away between the mangroves. Stories were told of an old make shift tum shack that was built to house pirates, salty dogs and sailors where they gathered to drink rum, chase wenches, eat grub and sing songs - yo-ho, yo-ho a pirates life for me!

Well not much has changed since then... They're all still right here... years have long gone by and the names have changed but the debauchery still continues day and night... and the little rum shack was just a distant memory.

About us

In 1995 hurricane Marilyn blew through St. Thomas destroying most of the island, including, the little gas station named "hearts." As the years past, the abandoned gas station caught my eye. I envisioned the old rum shack - Can you see what I saw? Guess what... now it's the home of "Big Bamboo burger & seafood shack" ... Surrounded by mangroves & palm trees, open air dockside dining doesn't get any better than this. It's an authentic island tiki shack - complete with palm thatch roog & Bamboo structure - serves uptropical fare by our native chefs. The open grills' aroma will beckon you to order our local chicken roti, fresh local catch, Caribbean lobster, Shrimp or one of our signiture burgers - in paradise that is! Big bamboo burger & seafood shack - it's what a tropical bar in paradise should be.... palm trees, Caribbean breeze, cold beers, island rum and mucis emanating in the background of Jimmy Buffet and Bob Marley.
